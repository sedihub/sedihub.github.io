Bessel's Correction

September 6, 2020

(This is one of my old posts from my old blog, Digressions.) Have you ever wondered why the sample variance of $n$ random variable has $n-1$ in the denominator instead of $n$? $$ \sigma^2_{n} = \frac{1}{\underbrace{\mathbf{n-1}}_{\text{instead of }\mathbf{n}}}\sum_{i=1}^{n} (X_{i} - \bar{X})^2, $$ where, $\bar{X}=\frac{1}{n}\sum_{i=1}^{n} X_i$ is the mean of the $n$ instances of the random variable. The reason for the appearance of $\frac{1}{n-1}$ instead of $\frac{1}{n}$ is that the above expression for variance is not the estimated variance and is the Bessel-corrected estimated variance.
